Monster Cake

Chocolate Sponge Cake with Chocolate Buttercream Frosting.

"It's said that once you have a taste of this cake, you'll never forget its sweetness."

Ingredients

For the chocolate cake:
  • 1 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 ½ cups sugar
  • 1 ½ cups flour
  • 1 ½ cups cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 ½ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • 1 ½ cup liquid of your choice (water, coffee, stout)
  • 1 Tbsp vanilla extract
  • ½ cup mayonnaise
  • 2 large eggs
For the chocolate buttercream frosting:
  • 1 cup salted butter
  • 3 ½ cups sifted powdered sugar
  • ½ cup cocoa powder
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup + 2-3 Tbsp heavy whipping cream
  • 1 cup finely chopped good quality chocolate

Directions

Prepare the cake:
  1. Preheat the oven to 350℉. Butter and flour 3 8-inch round cake pans.
  2. Cream together the butter and sugar on medium high speed for about 5 minutes until mixture is light in color and fluffy (medium high speed for about 5 minutes). I like to let this cream while doing the next few steps.
  3. In a separate bowl, sift flour, cocoa powder, salt, baking soda, and baking powder together in a large bowl.
  4. Mix your choice of liquid and the vanilla extract together in a small bowl.
  5. Once the butter mixture is creamed nicely, add the mayonnaise. Mix well.
  6. Add one egg at a time to the butter mixture, mixing just until each egg is incorporated in mixture (do not over mix).
  7. Add 1/3 dry mixture to butter mixture, beat until well incorporated.
  8. Add 1/2 wet mixture to butter mixture, beat until well incorporated.
  9. Repeat last two steps until all ingredients are incorporated (1/3 dry, ½ wet, 1/3 dry, ½ wet, 1/3 dry).
  10. Scrape sides and bottom and beat on medium high for 1-2 minutes, until mixture is smooth and consistent.
  11. Pour into prepared cake pans.
  12. Bake at 350℉ for 20-25min.
  13. Let cake cool completely before frosting.
Prepare the chocolate buttercream frosting:
  1. In a small saucepan, heat the 1/4 cup of heavy whipping cream until bubbling around the edge (heavy whipping cream will very quickly start to boil and overflow so do not leave unattended).
  2. Put chocolate (finely chopped) into a heat safe bowl and pour heated whipping cream over chocolate; allow to sit for about 3-5 minutes to melt chocolate. Mix together until smooth. Set aside to cool.
  3. In a stand mixer, cream butter for about 5 minutes; butter should appear shiny and fluffy.
  4. Sift powdered sugar and cocoa powder together in a separate bowl.
  5. Using a whisk attachment, add 1 cup of sifted powdered sugar/cocoa mixture at a time. Beat well in between each addition. Do this until consistency is about right (not too stiff, not too soft).
  6. Add vanilla.
  7. Turn mixer to a medium low speed and slowly add the cooled, melted chocolate. Once all chocolate has been added, turn speed back up to a medium high and whisk for 3 minutes.
  8. If the mixture is still too thick, add 1 tbsp of heavy whipping cream at a time until frosting is light and fluffy. For a more spreadable frosting add more whipping cream, for a more sturdy frosting use less.
