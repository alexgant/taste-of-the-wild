Fruitcake

White Sponge Cake, whipped cream frosting, fresh strawberry filling, and fresh fruit toppings.

"Making ample use of fruits found all over Hyrule, this cake is a must for celebrations."

Ingredients

For the sponge cake:
  • 2 cups flour
  • 4 large eggs (separate yolk and whites)
  • 1 ½ cups sugar
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1½ cup butter
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
For the strawberry filling:
  • 3 cups fresh strawberries, washed and dry, separated
  • 2 cups thickly sliced or whole if small
  • 1 cup medium diced
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 12 Tbsp sugar
For the fresh fruit topping:
  • 2 cups fresh fruit, sliced if needed, washed and dry (berries, apples, oranges, grapes, etc)
  • 2 Tbsp lime juice
For the whipped cream frosting:
  • 2 cups heavy whipping cream
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 ½ cups powdered sugar

Directions

Prepare the cake:
  1. Preheat the oven to 325℉. Butter and flour 2 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. Cream together the butter and sugar on medium high speed for about 5 minutes until mixture is light in color and fluffy (medium high speed for about 5 minutes). I like to let this cream while doing the next few steps.
  3. In a separate bowl, sift flour, salt, and baking powder together in a large bowl.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk egg whites and vanilla on high until stiff peaks form.
  5. Add a little bit of the dry mixture at a time to the butter mixture and mix until well incorporated.
  6. Fold flour and butter mixture into egg whites; do not mix or whisk, folding is essential as to not lose the air whipped into the egg whites.
  7. Pour into prepared cake pans.
  8. Bake at 325℉ for 20-25min.
  9. Let cake cool completely before assembling with filling and frosting. While cake cools – prepare strawberry filling and fruit topping. Wait to prepare frosting until cake is cool and ready to frost.
Prepare the strawberry filling:
  1. Put the smaller cut fresh strawberries, lemon juice, and sugar in a small sauce pan. Cook on medium heat, stirring occasionally until strawberries have broken down a bit and there is a good amount of sauce in the pan. Continue cooking and reduce until sauce is thick enough to coat the back of a spoon.
  2. Strain cooked berry mixture to remove chunks of strawberry and seeds. Allow to cool to room temperature.
  3. Place 2 cups of fresh washed strawberries in a medium bowl and pour cooled strawberry sauce over strawberries and mix. Refrigerate at least one hour. Can be made the day before.
Prepare the fresh fruit topping:
    Place clean, dry fruit in a bowl with the 2 Tbsp of lime juice and gently toss to coat.
Prepare the whipped cream frosting:
  1. Pour heaving whipping cream and vanilla into stand mixer bowl
  2. Turn mixer on medium low speed and add sifted powdered sugar 1/2cup at a time.
  3. Once sugar is incorporated, turn mixer on high and whip until stiff peaks form.
